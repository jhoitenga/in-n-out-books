Who was Shirley Jackson?

Shirley Jackson (1916-1965) was an American author renowned for her pioneering contributions to the horror and psychological thriller genres. Born in San Francisco, her works often explored the darker aspects of human nature and society. Jackson gained widespread recognition with her chilling short story "The Lottery," published in 1948, which sparked controversy and critical acclaim for its exploration of conformity and mob mentality. Her novels, including "The Haunting of Hill House" (1959) and "We Have Always Lived in the Castle" (1962), showcased her mastery of creating eerie atmospheric tension and complex characters. Jackson's distinctive storytelling has left an indelible mark on the world of literature, influencing countless writers in the realms of horror and suspense.
